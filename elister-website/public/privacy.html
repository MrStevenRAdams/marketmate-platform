    1. Introduction

    Marketmate Solutions Ltd ("MarketMate", "we", "us", or "our") is committed to protecting and respecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, share, and protect personal data when you:

    • Visit our website at marketmate.co.uk and any subdomains (the "Website");
    • Register for and use the MarketMate platform and associated services (the "Platform");
    • Communicate with us by email, telephone, or through contact forms;
    • Receive marketing communications from us.

    This policy is written to comply with the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR), the Data Protection Act 2018, and — where applicable — the EU General Data Protection Regulation (EU GDPR) and the Privacy and Electronic Communications Regulations 2003 (PECR).

    Plain English summary: We collect information about you to operate our platform and communicate with you. We do not sell your personal data. We do not use your data for advertising. We use reputable, GDPR-compliant third-party services to run our infrastructure. This policy explains all of this in detail.

    2. Data Controller

    The data controller responsible for your personal data is:

    Marketmate Solutions Ltd
    Registered in England and Wales
    Email: privacy@marketmate.co.uk

    If you have any questions about this policy or how we handle your data, please contact us at the address above. We aim to respond to all privacy enquiries within 30 days.

    3. Data We Collect

    3.1 Data you provide directly

    • Account registration: Full name, business email address, password (stored as a hashed credential), company name, telephone number, country.
    • Billing and subscription: Billing address, VAT registration number. Payment card details are collected and stored directly by our payment processor (Stripe) — we never store raw card numbers on our systems.
    • Contact and enquiry forms: Name, email, phone number, company, message content, channels and order volumes you disclose voluntarily.
    • Platform content: Product listings, SKUs, inventory records, purchase orders, supplier data, warehouse configurations, automation rules, and any other data you enter into the Platform.
    • Support communications: The content of any emails or messages you send us, including technical and account queries.

    3.2 Data collected automatically

    • Usage data: Pages visited, features accessed, buttons clicked, session duration, search queries within the Platform.
    • Technical data: IP address, browser type and version, operating system, device type, screen resolution, referring URL.
    • Log data: Server-side request logs including timestamps, HTTP method, endpoint path, response codes, and processing time. Logs are used for debugging, security monitoring, and infrastructure management.
    • Cookie data: See Section 13 (Cookies) for full details.

    3.3 Data received from third parties

    • Marketplace APIs: When you connect your marketplace accounts (e.g. Amazon Seller Central, eBay, Etsy), we retrieve order data, inventory levels, listing data, buyer messaging threads, and shipping information via those platforms' authorised APIs. This data is processed on your behalf as a data processor.
    • Carrier APIs: Shipment tracking data retrieved from carrier APIs (Royal Mail, Evri, DPD, FedEx) on your behalf.
    • AI service providers: When you use our AI listing generation features, product information is sent to Anthropic (Claude) and/or Google (Gemini) APIs. This data is used solely to generate listing content and is not retained by those providers for training purposes under our enterprise agreements.

    4. How Data Is Collected

    We collect data through the following means:

    • Direct submission: Via registration forms, contact forms, and in-platform data entry.
    • Automated technical systems: Via server logs, cookies, and analytics tools as described in this policy.
    • Third-party API calls: When you grant MarketMate authorisation to access your marketplace and carrier accounts via OAuth or API keys, we retrieve the relevant operational data on your behalf.
    • Payment processor: Stripe provides us with billing confirmation data (transaction ID, last four card digits, billing name) once payment is processed.

    6. Legal Basis for Processing

    We rely on the following legal bases under UK GDPR / EU GDPR:

    6.1 Contract performance (Article 6(1)(b))

    Processing necessary for the performance of the contract we have with you — including providing the Platform, processing your subscription, and sending service-critical communications.

    6.2 Legitimate interests (Article 6(1)(f))

    We process certain data where it is necessary for our legitimate business interests and those interests are not overridden by your rights. This includes: improving the Platform based on usage data, fraud detection, security monitoring, and sending relevant marketing to existing customers (soft opt-in under PECR). We have conducted Legitimate Interests Assessments (LIAs) for these processing activities.

    6.3 Legal obligation (Article 6(1)(c))

    Where we are required to process data to comply with applicable law — for example, retaining financial records under UK HMRC requirements, or responding to lawful data access requests from public authorities.

    6.4 Consent (Article 6(1)(a))

    Where you have given us explicit consent — for example, when subscribing to our newsletter via the website contact form, or when accepting non-essential cookies. You may withdraw consent at any time without affecting the lawfulness of processing before withdrawal.

    7. Marketplace and Third-Party Platform Data

    MarketMate integrates with third-party marketplace platforms including Amazon Seller Central, eBay, Etsy, OnBuy, Kaufland, Back Market, BigCommerce, Magento, WooCommerce, Bluepark, Temu, and TikTok Shop (collectively "Marketplace Platforms").

    7.1 Data we access on your behalf

    When you connect a Marketplace Platform account, MarketMate accesses data via that platform's authorised API. The categories of data accessed include:

    • Order details: order ID, order status, line items, quantities, prices, shipping addresses, requested shipping service
    • Buyer information: buyer name, delivery address, and (where provided by the platform) buyer message threads
    • Inventory levels and SKU data
    • Listing data: product titles, descriptions, images, prices, categories, attributes
    • Shipment and tracking information
    • Return and refund information

    This data is processed by MarketMate as your data processor — we act on your instructions as the data controller for this data. Our Data Processing Agreement (DPA) governs this relationship and is incorporated into our Terms of Service.

    7.2 Amazon SP-API

    MarketMate is an approved Amazon SP-API (Selling Partner API) application. Our integration is subject to Amazon's Developer Agreement and Data Protection Policy. We access only the data scopes required to provide our services, as documented in our SP-API application. Amazon buyer personal data accessed via SP-API is used solely to fulfil orders and provide related services to you as the seller — it is never used for our own commercial purposes, shared with third parties outside of what is necessary for order fulfilment, or retained beyond our stated retention periods.

    7.3 Buyer personal data

    Buyer names, delivery addresses, and contact information retrieved from marketplace platforms are processed on behalf of you (the seller) to enable order management, dispatch, and customer communications. This data:

    • Is stored securely within our UK-based cloud infrastructure;
    • Is not used for any purpose other than fulfilling the services you have requested;
    • Is not shared with any party other than shipping carriers (for label generation) and, where applicable, cloud infrastructure providers;
    • Is subject to the same retention and security standards as all other data on the Platform.

    7.4 AI listing generation

    When you use AI-powered listing generation, product information (titles, descriptions, attributes, categories) is transmitted to Anthropic (Claude) and/or Google (Gemini) APIs. This information:

    • Does not include buyer personal data;
    • Is used solely to generate listing content in response to your request;
    • Is subject to the data processing terms of our enterprise agreements with Anthropic and Google;
    • Is not retained by these providers for model training under our agreement terms.

    11. Security

    We implement technical and organisational security measures appropriate to the risk level of the data we process. These include:

    11.1 Technical measures

    • Encryption in transit: All data transmitted between your browser/application and our servers uses TLS 1.2 or higher (HTTPS). API communications are encrypted throughout.
    • Encryption at rest: All data stored in Firestore and associated Google Cloud storage is encrypted at rest using Google-managed encryption keys (AES-256).
    • Authentication: Platform accounts are protected by Firebase Authentication with support for multi-factor authentication (MFA).
    • Access controls: Role-based access control (RBAC) is implemented across the Platform. Staff access to production systems is restricted on a need-to-know basis and subject to access review.
    • Vulnerability management: Regular dependency updates, security scanning as part of our CI/CD pipeline, and periodic security reviews.
    • Monitoring and alerting: Automated monitoring for unusual access patterns, error spikes, and security events with on-call alerting for the engineering team.

    11.2 Organisational measures

    • Data protection is considered at the design stage of all new features (Privacy by Design and by Default).
    • Employees and contractors with access to personal data are bound by confidentiality obligations.
    • We maintain a documented Security Incident Response Plan governing how we detect, contain, assess, and notify in the event of a data breach.
    • We conduct periodic reviews of our data processing activities and this Privacy Policy.

    11.3 Data breach notification

    In the event of a personal data breach that is likely to result in a risk to your rights and freedoms, we will notify the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) within 72 hours of becoming aware of the breach, as required by UK GDPR Article 33. Where the breach is likely to result in a high risk to you, we will also notify you directly without undue delay.

    12. Your Rights

    Under UK GDPR (and EU GDPR where applicable), you have the following rights in respect of your personal data. We aim to respond to all rights requests within 30 calendar days. For complex requests, this may be extended by a further 60 days, in which case we will inform you.

    12.1 Right of access (Article 15)

    You have the right to obtain a copy of the personal data we hold about you, along with information about how it is used, where it came from, who it has been shared with, and how long we intend to keep it. This is also known as a Subject Access Request (SAR).

    12.2 Right to rectification (Article 16)

    You have the right to ask us to correct personal data that is inaccurate or incomplete. You may update much of your data directly within your account settings.

    12.3 Right to erasure (Article 17)

    You have the right to ask us to delete your personal data in certain circumstances — for example, where the data is no longer necessary for the purpose it was collected, or where you withdraw consent and there is no other legal basis for processing. This right is not absolute and may be subject to legal retention requirements.

    12.4 Right to restriction of processing (Article 18)

    You have the right to ask us to restrict processing of your data in certain circumstances — for example, while a dispute about accuracy is being resolved, or where processing is unlawful but you prefer restriction to erasure.

    12.5 Right to data portability (Article 20)

    Where processing is based on consent or contract and carried out by automated means, you have the right to receive your personal data in a structured, commonly used, machine-readable format (such as CSV or JSON), and to transmit it to another controller. The Platform provides built-in data export functionality for this purpose.

    12.6 Right to object (Article 21)

    You have the right to object to processing based on legitimate interests, including profiling. You also have an absolute right to object to direct marketing (including profiling for marketing purposes) at any time. Where you object, we will cease that processing unless we can demonstrate compelling legitimate grounds that override your rights.

    12.7 Rights related to automated decision-making (Article 22)

    MarketMate does not make decisions about you solely by automated means that produce legal or similarly significant effects. Where automation is used (e.g. in fraud detection), human review is available.

    12.8 Right to withdraw consent

    Where processing is based on your consent, you may withdraw that consent at any time by contacting us or using the unsubscribe mechanism in any marketing email. Withdrawal does not affect the lawfulness of processing before withdrawal.

    How to exercise your rights

    To exercise any of the above rights, please contact us at privacy@marketmate.co.uk with a description of your request. We may ask you to verify your identity before processing the request. We will not charge a fee for reasonable requests; however, we reserve the right to charge a reasonable administrative fee for manifestly unfounded or excessive requests, or to refuse them.
