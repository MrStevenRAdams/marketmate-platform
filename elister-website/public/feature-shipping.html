Carrier Features

Complete shipping workflow, end to end

⚖️ Live Rate Shopping

MarketMate queries all four carriers simultaneously when you open the shipping panel. Rates are live and reflect your negotiated carrier account pricing. Compare on cost, speed, and service level before committing.

🏷️ Label Generation & Printing

Generate carrier-compliant labels in PDF format directly in the platform. Print to Zebra thermal printers, Dymo label printers, or standard A4. Labels include all required barcodes, tracking numbers, and sender/recipient data.

📋 End-of-Day Manifests

Generate carrier collection manifests at the end of each dispatch day. Manifests are formatted to each carrier's requirements and can be emailed, printed, or exported. Manifests page gives you a full history of all carrier hand-offs.

📊 SLA Dashboard Q3 2026

Track your dispatch performance against each marketplace's SLA commitment in real time. See at-risk orders before they breach, analyse historical compliance rates, and identify which channels or product types cause the most SLA pressure.

⚠️ Delivery Exception Handling

When a carrier reports a delivery exception, MarketMate surfaces it in the Delivery Exceptions page linked to the original order. Acknowledge, investigate, and rebook from one place — then sync the updated status back to the marketplace.

🔄 Label Cancellation & Reprint

Cancel a generated label (within the carrier's cancellation window) and rebook with a different service or carrier. Reprint any label for any historical order without re-entering shipment details.

📦 Split Packaging

Split one order across multiple parcels when items are too large for a single shipment. Generate separate labels for each parcel, each with its own tracking number. All parcels are linked to the parent order throughout.

🚀 Dispatch Console

The batch dispatch console lets warehouse staff scan order barcodes to process, generate labels, and confirm dispatch in rapid succession — optimised for high-volume dispatch days without requiring mouse use.
